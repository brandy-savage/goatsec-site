The outcome

An evidence package that holds up under auditor scrutiny.

Most audit failures aren't about having bad security, they're about having good security that's poorly documented. Controls exist but aren't mapped to the framework. Policies are outdated. Evidence is scattered across Confluence, shared drives, and email threads. We fix the documentation layer so your actual security posture translates into audit success.

We've been through enough audits with enough clients to know exactly what auditors look for, and exactly where organizations waste time preparing for things that won't be tested while leaving gaps in what will.

When to use this offering
  • ✦ Your first SOC 2 or ISO 27001 audit is approaching and you don't know what gaps exist
  • ✦ You received qualifications on a previous audit and want help remediating cleanly
  • ✦ Enterprise customers are requiring you to complete security questionnaires you don't have documentation for
  • ✦ Your policies are more than two years old and your technology environment has changed significantly
  • ✦ You're operating in a regulated industry (healthcare, finance, government) and need framework-specific guidance

Deliverables

What you receive from a GRC engagement.

Core deliverables
  • ✦ Gap analysis report with control-level findings and remediation priority
  • ✦ Evidence collection package organized by framework requirement
  • ✦ Policy documentation: information security, acceptable use, access control, incident response, and others as required by your framework
  • ✦ Risk register: seeded with identified risks, categorized, and formatted for ongoing maintenance
  • ✦ Board security report template with KPIs tied to your specific framework
Inputs we need from you
  • ✦ Target framework and audit timeline
  • ✦ Existing policies and procedures documentation
  • ✦ Current technology inventory and architecture overview
  • ✦ Previous audit results or assessor findings if available
  • ✦ Contact for your compliance or legal team for policy review coordination
Auditor coordination

For clients approaching an active audit, we can coordinate directly with the auditor, managing the evidence request process, fielding technical questions, and representing your controls in conversations where a security practitioner's voice is more useful than a policy document's.
