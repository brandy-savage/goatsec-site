Executive track

Security fluency for the people making the decisions.

Board members, C-suite leaders, and senior managers don't need to understand how buffer overflows work. They need to understand how to evaluate security risk, make informed decisions under pressure, and lead an organization through a security incident without making it worse.

Our executive training is available as live workshops, tabletop facilitation, or pre-recorded modules, adapted to whatever format fits your leadership team's schedule and learning style.

Modules available
  • ✦ Threat landscape briefing: the attacks most likely to affect your industry, explained without jargon
  • ✦ Incident response tabletop: leadership-specific simulation of a breach or ransomware scenario
  • ✦ Decision-making under pressure: how to lead when a security incident is active
  • ✦ Social engineering and executive-targeted phishing: what attacks on leadership look like and how to recognize them
  • ✦ Security investment framework: how to evaluate security proposals and ask the right questions
  • ✦ Board-level security governance: roles, responsibilities, and oversight best practices

Training curriculum approach

Developer training isn't effective when it's abstract. We build curriculum around your actual stack, your actual codebase patterns, and the vulnerability classes most likely to appear in your specific type of application. Generic OWASP slides are a starting point, not the training.

Developer track

Security skills for the people writing the code.

Developer security training works when it's specific, practical, and delivered in a way that fits how developers actually learn. We build curriculum around your stack and your real vulnerability patterns, not generic slides that could apply to any company.

We also design and launch Security Champion programs, giving motivated developers on your team the knowledge and organizational support to be effective security advocates inside their own engineering group.

Modules available
  • ✦ Secure coding practices: OWASP Top 10 and beyond, with examples from your actual codebase
  • ✦ Threat modeling for engineers: how to apply STRIDE to features before they're built
  • ✦ API security: common patterns, common mistakes, and how to review your own API design
  • ✦ CI/CD security toolchain onboarding: how to use the security gates in your pipeline
  • ✦ Security champion program: design, launch, and ongoing enablement for developer security leads
  • ✦ Data protection and privacy: handling PII, encryption, and regulatory obligations in code
