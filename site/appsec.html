The outcome

Security that ships with your code, not after it.

The goal of application security isn't to generate a findings report every year, it's to make it harder to ship a vulnerable application in the first place. We design AppSec programs that integrate into your development workflow so that security becomes a default, not an afterthought.

After working with us, your team knows how to threat model a feature before it's built, your pipeline catches common vulnerability classes before they reach production, and your security posture has a documented baseline you can improve against.

When to use this offering
  • ✦ You've never had a formal AppSec assessment or program
  • ✦ You're preparing for SOC 2, ISO 27001, or a customer audit requiring AppSec evidence
  • ✦ Your scanner produces hundreds of findings with no prioritization logic
  • ✦ You're scaling your engineering team and security isn't keeping up
  • ✦ A recent pen test found things that your internal process should have caught
When this is not the right fit

If you need a black-box penetration test with a compliance checkbox as the only goal, there are faster and cheaper options. Our AppSec work is designed for teams that want a durable program, not a one-time report.

Methodology

Our AppSec assessments are grounded in the OWASP Application Security Verification Standard (ASVS). We assess against the level appropriate for your application classification, payment processing, healthcare data, and SaaS platforms typically require different levels of rigor.

For threat modeling, we use STRIDE as a starting framework and adapt to the architectural patterns we see. Every threat model is documented in a format your team can maintain and update as the application evolves.

Inputs we need from you
  • ✦ Access to code repositories or architecture documentation
  • ✦ Existing SDLC documentation if available
  • ✦ Recent scan results from any existing tooling
  • ✦ Deployment architecture overview (cloud provider, services used)
  • ✦ Time with a developer or architect for threat modeling sessions (typically two hours)
