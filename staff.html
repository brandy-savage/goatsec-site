Why it's different

A referral from someone who has worked alongside them.

Most staffing processes work the same way: a recruiter screens résumés against a keyword list, passes candidates to you, and collects a fee when someone gets hired. The problem is that security talent is hard to evaluate on paper. A résumé that says "penetration testing" and "SIEM engineering" can mean almost anything.

Our network is built differently. Every practitioner we place is someone we've worked with on a real engagement, someone whose technical depth we've seen firsthand, whose judgment under pressure we've observed, and whose communication style we can vouch for. We're not placing strangers; we're extending our own reputation.

That means a GoatSec introduction comes with context you won't get from a staffing agency: what they're genuinely great at, where they work best, what environment suits their style, and what you should ask them in the interview to see their best work.

When to use staff augmentation
  • ✦ You need specific technical depth for a time-boxed project. AppSec for a product launch, detection engineering for a SOC build, GRC for an audit cycle
  • ✦ You've had a departure or unexpected gap and need bridge coverage while you run a full-time search
  • ✦ You're a retainer client and need an embedded resource for a specific workstream
  • ✦ You want to evaluate someone for a full-time role with a contract-to-hire arrangement

How it works

From your description to an introduction in days, not weeks.

1
Tell us what you need

Role description, technical depth required, timeline, environment overview, and whether this is project-based or ongoing. The more context you give us, the better the match.

2
We match from our network

We identify one to three practitioners from our network who fit the technical and contextual requirements. We brief you on each, not just their résumé, but our firsthand read on their strengths and style.

3
Introduction and interview

We facilitate the introduction and can advise on interview approach for the specific specialization. We're available to help structure a technical assessment if useful.

4
Engagement starts

For retainer clients, resources can be embedded directly into active GoatSec workstreams. For standalone placements, we hand off cleanly with a brief to the incoming resource on the environment and priorities.

Engagement structures

Flexible arrangements for how your need is actually shaped.

Project-based
Defined scope, defined end

A practitioner engaged for a specific project, an assessment sprint, an integration build, an audit cycle. Clear start and end dates. Deliverables defined before work begins.

Typical length: two to twelve weeks depending on scope.

Ongoing capacity
Part-time or full-time for a defined period

A practitioner working alongside your team on an ongoing basis, a set number of hours per week, for a defined term. Common for teams with a backlog that needs consistent bandwidth to work down.

Typical length: three to six months with optional renewal.

Retainer-embedded
Integrated into a GoatSec retainer

For Executive Retainer clients, additional practitioners can be embedded into active workstreams. GoatSec coordinates the resources, you get a single engagement with coherent execution across all active work.

Available as an add-on to any active retainer engagement.
